Shawwal


The month of Shawwal is the tenth lunar month of the Islamic calendar. Shawwal was named during the pre-Islamic era and its name is said to be derived from the shala (to become sparse) as it was the time in which camel's milk was in short supply due to extreme heat. Among the virtues of Shawwal is that it contains Eid al-Fitr and marks the beginning of the hajj season.
Devotions to observe during Shawwal:
  • Observing 'Eid al-Fitr (click on date highlighted in green for more details).
  • Distributing zakat al-fitr
  • Fasting the first six days of Shawwal (click on date highlighted in brown for more details).
  • To make one's way to hajj.
Historical events that took place in Shawwal:
  • The Prophet's (saw) marriage to Aisha (ra).
  • The birth of 'Abdullah bin Zubayr (ra).
  • The Battle of Bani Sulaym (2 AH).
  • The Battle of Bani Qaynuqa (2 AH).
  • The Battle of Uhud (3 AH).
  • The Battle of the Trench (5 AH).
  • The Battle of Hunayn (8 AH).
  • The Battle of Ta'if (8 AH).
  • The birth of al-Imam al-Bukhari (194 AH).

Fasting on Monday


Abu Qatada reported that the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, was asked about fasting Mondays and he said, "That is the day on which I was born and the day when I was sent - or when revelation descended on me." (Muslim)
Abu Hurayra reported that the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, said, "Actions are presented on Mondays and Thursdays, so I like my actions to be presented while I am fasting." (Muslim related it without mentioning fasting.) (at-Tirmidhi)
'A'isha said, "The Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, used to take care to fast on Mondays and Thursdays." (at-Tirmidhi)

Friday (al-Jumu'ah)

It was narrated from Aws bin Aws that : The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "One of the best of your days is Friday. On this day, Adam was created and on this day he died, on this day the Trumpet will be blown, and on this day all creatures will swoon. So send a great deal of salah upon me on this day, for your salah will be presented to me." They said: "O Messenger of Allah (ﷺ), how will our salah be presented to you when you have decomposed (after death)?" He said: "Allah (SWT), the Mighty and Sublime, has forbidden the earth to consume the bodies of the prophets, peace be upon them."
Merits of Friday

  • The Friday prayer. Regarding the Friday prayer Allah Most High said: "O you who believe, when the call for prayer is made on Friday, hasten, set off, to the remembrance of God, to the prayer, and leave aside [all] commerce, suspend [all] such contracts. That is better for you, should you know, that it is better for you, then do it" (62:9).

    Some recommended acts of attending the Friday prayer:
    • Taking a purificatory bath
    • Arriving early
    • Listening attentively to the Friday sermons
    • Reciting Quran and making remembrance of Allah Most High

  • Performing the dawn prayer with a group on Friday
  • The one who dies on Friday will be saved from the trials of the grave.

Eid al-Fitr


The Eid al-Fitr prayer is one of the best optional prayers. The prayer itself is recommended according to al-Imam al-Shafi'i, but other imams, such as Imam Abu Hanifah considered it obligatory. The time for the eid prayer enters after sunrise and lasts until noon; however, it is best to delay its performance until the sun has risen a spear's length in the sky. Some other recommended features of the eid pray include:
  • Performing the prayer in a masjid.
  • Spending the night leading upto eid in worship.
  • Performing the purificatory bath
  • Wearing perfume (for men) and nice clothing
  • To arrive early and to travel on foot.
  • To eat something before performing the eid prayer
How is the prayer peformed?
The eid prayer consists of two raka'ats. In the first rakat, it is recommened to make seven takbirs (excluding the opening takbir of the prayer) after reading the opening supplication, and to make five takbirs in the second rakat. It is recommended to raise one's hands for each takbir, and to read: "Glory be to Allah, all praise belongs to Allah, there is no god but Allah, Allah is the greatest" after each takbir. After the prayer is finished, if one is performing the prayer with a group, it is recommended that the prayer be followed by two sermons (similar to the Friday sermons).
Zakat al-Fitr:
It is obligatory to give zakat al-fitr in the Shafi'i school. It can be distributed anytime during Ramadhan but it must be given before sunset on the day of eid. However, it is before to give zakat al-fitr right before performing the eid prayer. Zakat al-fitr consists of giving 2.75 kilos of the staple food of the land for oneself and everyone else one is obliged to support (wife, children, etc) provided one's money exceeds one's expenses.

Beginning of the six days of Shawwal


The Prophet (saw) said, “Fasting Ramadan and following it with six days from Shawwal is like continual fasting.” It is recommended to observe fasting on the six days of Shawwal due to numerous hadiths indicating its recommended nature. According to the Shafi'i scholars, it is best to fast the six days of Shawwal consecutively.

White Days


Abu Dharr reported that the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, said, "If you fast three days of the month, then fast the thirteenth, fourteenth and fifteenth." (at-Tirmidhi)
Qatada ibn Milhan said, "The Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, used to command us to fast the white days: the thirteenth, fourteenth and fifteenth." (Abu Dawud)
